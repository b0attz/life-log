# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Anyone who wants a fast, simple personal journal. Thai-first locale, accessible globally. Used on desktop and mobile as a daily habit — logging thoughts, tracking moods, organizing entries into folders, and setting goals. No onboarding friction, no learning curve.

## Product Purpose

Replace heavyweight journaling tools (Notion, Day One) with a single-page app that loads instantly, works offline, and stays out of the way. Success is a user who opens the app, writes, and closes it — three taps, zero friction.

## Brand Personality

Clean, Fast, Simple

## Anti-references

- **Notion**: overwhelming for a journal. Nested pages, databases, templates — too much ceremony for a daily thought.
- **Day One**: feature bloat. Photos, videos, locations, weather, music — all noise when you just want to write.
- **Any app with a tutorial**: if it needs explaining, it's too complex.

## Design Principles

1. **Speed is a feature** — every interaction must feel instant. Loading states are a last resort, not a default.
2. **Strip to essence** — show only what the user needs right now. Progressive disclosure over feature dumps.
3. **Thai-first, global-ready** — Thai locale is the default experience, but the UI never breaks for non-Thai users.
4. **Earned complexity** — new features must prove they reduce friction, not add options.
5. **Offline-resilient** — the app should work when the network doesn't. Local-first, sync-when-ready.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- All interactive elements are keyboard-accessible (Ctrl+Enter to save, Escape to close dialogs)
- 5 themes with sufficient contrast ratios across light/dark/sepia/forest/ocean
- Reduced motion support via `prefers-reduced-motion`
- Touch-friendly targets (44px minimum)
- No color-only indicators — mood and state communicated through emoji and text alongside color
